How to Get Cheap Car Insurance That Covers Roadside Emergencies

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Let’s be real: nobody wakes up excited to shop for car insurance. But in today’s world—marked by economic uncertainty, climate-related disasters, and supply chain hiccups—having the right car insurance isn’t just a legal formality. It’s a financial lifeline. And when your battery dies on a deserted road during a storm, that lifeline better include roadside assistance.

The good news? You don’t have to break the bank to get it. With the right strategy, you can find a policy that’s both affordable and comprehensive, covering everything from a simple tow to a lockout service. This guide will walk you through the steps to secure cheap car insurance that includes robust roadside emergency coverage.

Why Roadside Assistance is Non-Negotiable in 2024

Think about the last time you saw a news segment about extreme weather. Wildfires, floods, polar vortices—these aren’t futuristic scenarios; they’re today’s headlines. Climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of weather events that can leave drivers stranded. A reliable roadside assistance plan is no longer a luxury; it’s a critical component of modern driving safety.

Beyond weather, consider economic pressures. With inflation impacting everything from grocery bills to auto parts, an unexpected $200 tow bill or a $100 jump-start can derail your monthly budget. Rolling this coverage into your insurance policy is often far cheaper than paying for these services out-of-pocket or through a standalone club membership.

Why Your Current Policy Might Not Be Enough

Many drivers assume they have roadside assistance because they have “full coverage.” This is a dangerous misconception. “Full coverage” typically refers to a combination of liability, comprehensive, and collision coverage. It does not automatically include emergency road services. This is an add-on, often called a “rider” or “endorsement,” that you must specifically request and pay a little extra for.

Strategies for Finding the Cheapest Comprehensive Policy

Finding a great deal on car insurance that includes roadside help isn’t about magic. It’s about methodology. Here’s how to work the system in your favor.

1. Bundle Your Policies (The Power of One)

The easiest way to save significant money is by bundling your auto insurance with other policies, most commonly your homeowners or renters insurance. Insurance companies reward customer loyalty with multi-policy discounts, often saving you 10% to 25% overall. When you bundle, ask to add the roadside assistance endorsement to your auto policy. The savings from the bundle can effectively make the roadside coverage free or extremely low-cost.

2. Increase Your Deductible Wisely

Your deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket before your insurance kicks in for a claim. Opting for a higher deductible on your comprehensive and collision coverage will lower your premium. The key is to set the deductible to an amount you can comfortably afford in an emergency. The savings on your premium can then be reallocated to add roadside assistance, which typically has no deductible when you use it.

3. Don’t Just Settle for the Default Roadside Coverage

Not all roadside assistance packages are created equal. When comparing quotes, dig into the details.

  • Towing Distance: Does it cover towing to the nearest mechanic, or only 5 miles? Look for policies that offer longer distances (e.g., 100 miles).
  • Service Limits: Are there limits on how many times you can use it per year? A good policy will offer 4-5 incidents per year.
  • What’s Included: Standard services are towing, jump-starts, flat tire changes, lockout service, and fuel delivery. Ensure all are included.

Sometimes, a slightly more expensive policy has a vastly superior roadside program, making it a better value.

4. Leverage Technology and Telematics

Usage-based insurance (UBI) programs, like Progressive’s Snapshot or Allstate’s Drivewise, use a telematics device or mobile app to track your driving habits. Safe driving—smooth braking, limited nighttime driving, low mileage—can earn you substantial discounts. If you’re a safe driver, enrolling in one of these programs can slash your premium, freeing up cash to add roadside coverage. It’s a win-win: you drive safer and get protected for less.

5. Ask About Every Possible Discount

Insurance companies offer dozens of discounts that they won’t always apply automatically. You have to ask. Common discounts include:

  • Good Driver Discount: For a clean driving record.
  • Good Student Discount: For young drivers with good grades.
  • Pay-in-Full Discount: Pay your six-month or annual premium all at once.
  • Paperless/Auto-Pay Discount: For going green and setting up automatic payments.
  • Defensive Driving Discount: For completing an approved course.
  • Occupation-Based Discounts: For certain professions like teachers, engineers, or government employees.

Every discount you qualify for brings down the overall cost, making add-ons like roadside assistance more affordable.

6. Shop Around Religiously (And Time It Right)

Loyalty doesn’t always pay. The market changes, and new customer incentives are often better than renewal rates. Make it a habit to get quotes from at least three different insurers every 12-18 months. Use independent insurance agents who can quote from multiple companies at once. The best time to shop is 2-3 weeks before your current policy expires. This gives you enough time to compare without feeling rushed.

When you get a quote, be sure to request that roadside assistance be included in each one so you’re comparing apples to apples.

What to Do When You Need to Use Your Roadside Assistance

You’ve secured the perfect policy. Now what? When disaster strikes, stay calm and follow these steps.

Step 1: Safety First

If possible, move your vehicle to the shoulder or a safe area away from traffic. Turn on your hazard lights. If it’s dark, inclement weather, or an unsafe location, stay inside your vehicle with the doors locked.

Step 2: Locate Your Insurance Information

Use your insurer’s mobile app. Most major insurers have apps that store your policy details and have a one-touch button to call for roadside help. This is the fastest and most efficient method. If you don’t have the app, call the number on your insurance card.

Step 3: Be Specific and Patient

Clearly tell the operator your location, the make and model of your car, your license plate number, and the nature of the problem (e.g., “flat tire,” “engine won’t turn over”). They will dispatch a local service provider and give you an estimated time of arrival. This can take time, especially during widespread bad weather, so be patient.

Step 4: Know What’s Covered

Remember the details of your coverage. Most policies will cover the cost of the service call itself, but you may be responsible for the cost of parts or materials. For example, if you run out of gas, the service to deliver the gas is covered, but you will likely have to pay for the fuel itself. If you need a tow, confirm that the driver is taking you to a location within your policy’s mileage limit.

By understanding that roadside assistance is a negotiable and affordable add-on, you can build a car insurance policy that doesn’t just protect you from catastrophic losses but also from the everyday annoyances and emergencies that can happen to any driver. In an unpredictable world, this kind of preparedness is the ultimate form of savings.
