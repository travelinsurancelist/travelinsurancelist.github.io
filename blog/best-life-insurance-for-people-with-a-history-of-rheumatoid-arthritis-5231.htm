Best Life Insurance for People with a History of Rheumatoid Arthritis

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Living with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) can be challenging, but securing the right life insurance shouldn’t be one of them. While RA is a chronic autoimmune condition that affects joints and overall health, it doesn’t automatically disqualify you from obtaining life insurance. In fact, many insurers offer tailored policies for individuals with RA, though rates and coverage options vary. This guide explores the best life insurance options for people with a history of rheumatoid arthritis, how underwriting works, and tips to improve your chances of approval.

Understanding Rheumatoid Arthritis and Life Insurance

Rheumatoid arthritis is more than just joint pain—it’s a systemic condition that can impact heart health, lung function, and overall longevity. Insurers assess RA applicants based on:

  • Disease severity: Mild, moderate, or severe RA.
  • Treatment plan: Medications like DMARDs, biologics, or steroids.
  • Complications: Presence of comorbidities like cardiovascular disease.
  • Stability: How well the condition is managed.

Because RA increases long-term health risks, insurers may charge higher premiums or impose exclusions. However, with proper documentation and a stable health record, affordable coverage is possible.

Types of Life Insurance Available for RA Patients

1. Term Life Insurance

Term life insurance provides coverage for a set period (e.g., 10–30 years). It’s often the most affordable option for RA patients, especially if the condition is well-controlled.

Pros:
- Lower premiums than permanent policies.
- Flexibility to choose coverage length.

Cons:
- No cash value accumulation.
- Renewal may be costly if health declines.

2. Whole Life Insurance

Whole life insurance offers lifelong coverage with a cash value component. It’s more expensive but guarantees acceptance (if approved) and fixed premiums.

Best for:
- RA patients who want lifelong security.
- Those who can afford higher premiums.

3. Guaranteed Issue Life Insurance

If RA is severe or poorly managed, guaranteed issue policies (no medical exam required) may be the only option. These come with lower coverage limits and higher costs.

Drawbacks:
- High premiums for minimal coverage.
- Graded death benefits (full payout only after 2–3 years).

4. Simplified Issue Life Insurance

A middle ground between traditional and guaranteed issue policies, simplified issue skips the medical exam but asks health questions. Approval depends on RA severity.

How Underwriters Evaluate RA Applicants

Insurers classify RA applicants into risk categories:

Preferred Plus/Best Rates

  • Mild RA with no joint deformities.
  • No biologics or steroid use.
  • Stable for 5+ years.

Standard Rates

  • Moderate RA with occasional flares.
  • On DMARDs (e.g., methotrexate) but no recent hospitalizations.

Substandard/High-Risk Rates

  • Severe RA with joint damage.
  • History of cardiovascular or lung complications.
  • Recent steroid dependency.

Tips to Secure Affordable Life Insurance with RA

1. Work with a Specialized Broker

Independent brokers who specialize in high-risk cases can match you with RA-friendly insurers like Prudential, Mutual of Omaha, or Banner Life.

2. Provide Detailed Medical Records

Submit:
- Rheumatologist reports.
- Treatment history (medications, physical therapy).
- Lab results (CRP, ESR levels).

3. Show Stability

Insurers favor applicants with:
- Consistent treatment compliance.
- No recent ER visits or flares.
- Healthy lifestyle (non-smoker, balanced diet, exercise).

4. Consider Group Insurance

Employer-sponsored or association group plans may offer coverage without medical underwriting.

Top Insurers for RA Patients

| Insurer | Best For | Key Feature |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Prudential | Moderate RA | Flexible underwriting |
| Mutual of Omaha | Mild RA | Competitive term rates |
| AIG | Severe RA | High-risk specialty |
| Banner Life | Stable RA | Low premiums for well-managed cases |

Final Thoughts

While rheumatoid arthritis complicates life insurance applications, it doesn’t make coverage impossible. By understanding underwriting criteria, comparing policies, and demonstrating disease management, RA patients can find affordable protection. Start early, gather thorough documentation, and consult experts to navigate the process smoothly. Your health journey shouldn’t prevent you from securing your family’s future.
