Best Low-Cost Car Insurance for Single Moms in Your State

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Being a single mom comes with its own set of challenges—juggling work, childcare, and finances can feel overwhelming. One of the biggest expenses? Car insurance. With rising inflation and economic uncertainty, finding affordable coverage is more critical than ever. The good news is that there are ways to secure reliable car insurance without breaking the bank.

Why Car Insurance Costs More for Single Moms

Single moms often face higher insurance premiums due to several factors:

1. Gender and Marital Status Bias

Historically, insurers have considered single women—especially those with dependents—as higher-risk drivers. While some states have banned gender-based pricing, others still factor it into premiums.

2. Credit Score Impact

Many insurers use credit scores to determine rates. Single moms managing tight budgets may have lower credit scores, leading to higher premiums.

3. Driving Habits

Single parents often drive more—school drop-offs, errands, and work commutes add up. More miles mean higher risk in the eyes of insurers.

How to Find the Cheapest Car Insurance

1. Compare Quotes from Multiple Providers

Never settle for the first quote you get. Use online comparison tools to check rates from companies like Geico, Progressive, State Farm, and local insurers.

2. Ask About Discounts

Many insurers offer discounts for:
- Good student discounts (if your teen is on the policy)
- Safe driver programs (using telematics devices)
- Bundling policies (combining auto and renters/home insurance)
- Military or employer-affiliated discounts

3. Adjust Your Coverage

If you’re driving an older car, consider dropping collision coverage. Liability-only policies are much cheaper.

4. Improve Your Credit Score

Paying bills on time and reducing debt can help lower your insurance costs over time.

Best Low-Cost Car Insurance Companies for Single Moms

1. Geico

Known for competitive rates, Geico offers discounts for federal employees, military personnel, and good students. Their mobile app makes managing payments easy.

2. Progressive

Progressive’s Snapshot program rewards safe driving with lower premiums. They also offer name-your-price tools to fit tight budgets.

3. State Farm

Great for personalized service, State Farm agents can help tailor a policy to your needs. They also offer drive-safe discounts and multi-policy savings.

4. USAA (For Military Families)

If you’re a veteran or active-duty service member, USAA provides some of the lowest rates and best customer service in the industry.

5. Local and Regional Insurers

Smaller companies like Erie Insurance or Auto-Owners often have lower overhead costs, translating to cheaper premiums.

Government Assistance Programs

Some states offer low-income assistance programs to help single moms afford car insurance:
- California’s Low-Cost Auto Insurance Program (CLCA)
- New Jersey’s Special Automobile Insurance Policy (SAIP)
Check your state’s DMV website for similar programs.

Final Tips to Save Even More

  • Increase your deductible (if you have emergency savings).
  • Take a defensive driving course (some insurers offer discounts).
  • Drive a safer, older car (newer models cost more to insure).
  • Pay annually instead of monthly (avoid installment fees).

Being a single mom doesn’t mean you have to overpay for car insurance. With the right strategies, you can find affordable coverage that keeps you and your family protected.
