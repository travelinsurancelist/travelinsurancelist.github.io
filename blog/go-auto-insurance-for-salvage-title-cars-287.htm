Go Auto Insurance for Salvage Title Cars

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Navigating the world of auto insurance can be tricky, especially when dealing with salvage title cars. These vehicles, often labeled as "totaled" by insurance companies, come with unique challenges and opportunities. Whether you're a budget-conscious buyer, a DIY enthusiast, or someone looking for an affordable ride, understanding how to insure a salvage title car is crucial.

What Is a Salvage Title Car?

A salvage title is issued when a vehicle has been deemed a total loss by an insurance company. This typically happens when the cost of repairs exceeds a certain percentage (usually 70-75%) of the car’s actual cash value (ACV). Common reasons include:

  • Major accidents – Severe collision damage
  • Natural disasters – Flood, fire, or hail damage
  • Theft recovery – Vehicles recovered after being stolen but with significant damage

Once repaired and inspected, a salvage title can be "rebuilt" or "reconstructed," allowing the car to be legally driven again. However, insuring it remains a challenge.

Why Consider a Salvage Title Car?

Despite the stigma, salvage title cars can be a smart choice for certain buyers:

Cost Savings

Salvage cars are significantly cheaper than their clean-title counterparts. If you’re willing to handle repairs or buy a professionally rebuilt vehicle, you can save thousands.

Project Car Potential

For mechanics and car enthusiasts, salvage titles offer a low-cost way to acquire vehicles for restoration or customization.

Short-Term Use

If you need a temporary or secondary vehicle, a salvage car can be a practical option—just don’t expect top resale value.

Challenges of Insuring a Salvage Title Car

While the savings are appealing, insurance companies view salvage title cars as high-risk. Here’s why:

Limited Coverage Options

Many insurers refuse to offer full coverage (comprehensive and collision) for salvage title cars. Some may only provide liability insurance, which covers damage you cause to others but not your own vehicle.

Higher Premiums

Even if you find an insurer willing to cover a salvage car, premiums may be higher due to perceived risks.

Strict Inspection Requirements

Before insuring a rebuilt salvage car, insurers may require a thorough inspection to ensure it’s roadworthy.

How to Get Auto Insurance for a Salvage Title Car

Despite the hurdles, securing insurance is possible with the right approach:

1. Shop Around for Specialty Insurers

Not all insurance companies treat salvage titles the same. Some, like Go Auto Insurance, may offer more flexible policies for rebuilt vehicles. Look for insurers specializing in high-risk or non-standard auto coverage.

2. Provide Documentation

Insurers will want proof that the car has been properly repaired. Be prepared to submit:
- Rebuilt title paperwork
- Repair receipts
- Mechanic’s inspection report

3. Consider Liability-Only Insurance

If full coverage isn’t available, liability insurance is the minimum legal requirement in most states. While it won’t cover your car’s damage, it protects you from financial liability in an accident.

4. Explore Usage-Based Insurance

Some modern insurers offer pay-per-mile or usage-based policies, which could be a cost-effective option if you don’t drive often.

The Future of Salvage Title Cars in a Changing Auto Market

With rising car prices and supply chain disruptions, salvage title cars are gaining attention. Here’s how current trends impact their viability:

Electric Vehicles (EVs) and Salvage Titles

As EVs become more common, their salvage market grows. However, battery repairs can be costly, making some insurers hesitant to cover them.

Self-Driving Cars and Salvage Risks

Autonomous vehicles involved in accidents may end up with salvage titles. Insurers are still figuring out how to assess risk for these high-tech rebuilds.

Sustainability and the Salvage Market

With growing emphasis on sustainability, repairing and reusing salvage cars aligns with eco-friendly practices—potentially making them more mainstream.

Final Tips for Salvage Title Car Buyers

If you’re considering a salvage title car, keep these tips in mind:
- Get a professional inspection before buying.
- Check state laws—some states have strict rules for rebuilt titles.
- Compare insurance quotes before finalizing a purchase.

While salvage title cars aren’t for everyone, they can be a smart choice with the right preparation. By understanding the insurance landscape, you can make an informed decision and potentially save big on your next vehicle.
